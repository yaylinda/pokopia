## Design Context

### Users
Pokopia is primarily used by the project owner and by casual Pokopia players who want a faster way to browse game information than a spreadsheet. The app is used as a lightweight companion while playing or planning, with the main job being easy reference and quick comparison across Pokemon and habitats.

### Brand Personality
The interface should feel like a game helper and comparison companion rather than a spreadsheet viewer or admin dashboard. It should be approachable, readable, light-first, and clearly rooted in Pokemon-style playfulness without becoming childish or cluttered.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a polished fan-guide direction inspired by the game's visual language rather than a literal recreation of the in-game UI. Favor a bright light mode, collectible energy, strong iconography, and a tactile field-guide feel with richer character art, type/specialty signaling, and surfaces that support scanning and comparison.

### Design Principles
1. Make lookup feel immediate: surface the most useful comparison cues first.
2. Keep the app light and playful: bright, readable, and game-adjacent rather than dashboard-like.
3. Lean into collectible identity: use Pokemon imagery, category iconography, and visual grouping to make entries memorable.
4. Preserve static-site simplicity: no design choice should fight the repo's static React architecture.
5. Favor differentiation over sameness: avoid generic card grids, default SaaS patterns, and flat utility styling.
